Protein Measurement with the
Folin Phenol Reagent

Lowry, Rosebrough, Farr & Randall · J. Biol. Chem. 193:265-275 · 1951

A simple, sensitive, two-step colorimetric assay for measuring protein concentration. Quietly published, rarely cited in its first decade — and now with more than 310,000 citations, the most-cited scientific paper of all time.

Open paper → Biochemistry Method paper

TL;DR

A protein sample reacts first with copper(II) ions in alkaline solution, then with the Folin-Ciocalteu (phenol) reagent. The resulting deep-blue colour is read at 750 nm and converted to a protein concentration. Cheap, sensitive down to a few μg, and adopted by virtually every wet lab in the second half of the 20th century.

Why so heavily cited?

  • Universal need
    Quantifying protein is the first step in nearly every biochemistry experiment.
  • Cheap & simple
    Two reagents, basic glassware, a colorimeter — nothing exotic.
  • Sensitive
    Detects μg-level protein, far below the older biuret method.
  • Citation custom
    Every paper using the assay cited Lowry — for decades.
