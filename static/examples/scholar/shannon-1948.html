Shannon's communication system

A source emits a message, the transmitter encodes it, the channel adds noise, the receiver decodes, and the destination reads it.

Two key equations

Entropy of a source
$$H(X) = -\sum_i p_i \log_2 p_i$$
The minimum bits needed, on average, to encode each message — the deepest fact in compression.
Channel capacity
$$C = B \log_2\!\left(1 + \tfrac{S}{N}\right)$$
The maximum error-free bit-rate over a noisy channel, in terms of bandwidth $B$ and signal-to-noise ratio $S/N$.

Why Shannon matters

  • Compression has a floor
    Below entropy, lossless coding is impossible. ZIP, JPEG, MP3, H.264 — all fight Shannon's limit.
  • Noise can be defeated
    Below the capacity, error-free communication is achievable — basis of all modern modems and Wi-Fi.
  • Information ≠ meaning
    Shannon's bit measures uncertainty, not semantics — a clean separation that birthed digital culture.
