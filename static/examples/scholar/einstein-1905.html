The constancy of the speed of light

Two observers — one stationary, one moving with the train — both measure the same speed for the photon. That insistence forces space and time to bend instead.

Three iconic equations

Lorentz factor
$$\gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - v^2/c^2}}$$
Time dilation
$$\Delta t' = \gamma\,\Delta t$$
Length contraction
$$L' = L/\gamma$$

All three are consequences of the two postulates and the speed of light being invariant.

Counterintuitive predictions

  • Moving clocks run slow
    Verified to high precision with atomic clocks on aircraft and GPS satellites.
  • Lengths contract
    Objects shorten along the direction of motion as $v \to c$.
  • Simultaneity is relative
    Events that are simultaneous in one frame need not be in another.
  • Mass-energy equivalence
    $E=mc^2$ followed in a 3-page sequel later that same year.

Lorentz factor as you approach light speed

$\gamma$ stays close to 1 until $v$ exceeds about 50% of $c$ — then it explodes.
