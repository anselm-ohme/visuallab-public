ImageNet Classification with Deep CNNs

Alex Krizhevsky, Ilya Sutskever, Geoffrey Hinton · NIPS 2012 · University of Toronto

A 60-million-parameter convolutional neural network — trained on two GTX 580 GPUs — slashed the ImageNet top-5 error from 26.2% to 15.3% and triggered the modern deep-learning era.

The 8-layer architecture

Five conv layers + three fully-connected layers, with ReLU non-linearities and dropout for regularization.

Innovations introduced

  • ReLU activations
    Trained ~6× faster than the saturating tanh used previously.
  • Dropout regularization
    Random unit removal at training time prevents overfitting.
  • Local Response Normalization
    Lateral inhibition between channels boosted accuracy.
  • Two-GPU training
    Filters split across two GTX 580s with cross-GPU sync — practical large-scale GPU training.

The shockwave

  • Industry hire
    Hinton, Krizhevsky & Sutskever joined Google four months later via DNNresearch acquisition.
  • CNNs go viral
    VGG, GoogLeNet, ResNet, EfficientNet and many more all built on AlexNet's template.
  • GPU revolution
    NVIDIA pivoted from gaming to AI; market cap grew ~80× by 2024.
