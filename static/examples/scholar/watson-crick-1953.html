Five claims that built modern biology

  1. 1 · Two helical chains
    DNA is two strands wound around a common axis, each a right-handed helix.
  2. 2 · Antiparallel
    The two strands run in opposite 5′→3′ directions.
  3. 3 · Sugar-phosphate backbone
    Bases point inward; phosphates are on the outside.
  4. 4 · Specific base pairing
    A pairs with T, G pairs with C — the rule that explains heredity.
  5. 5 · A copying mechanism
    "It has not escaped our notice that the specific pairing we have postulated immediately suggests a possible copying mechanism for the genetic material." — perhaps the most understated sentence in 20th-century science.
