The Beatles

English rock band formed in Liverpool in 1960. Comprising John Lennon, Paul McCartney, George Harrison, and Ringo Starr, they became the best-selling music act of all time and reshaped 20th-century popular culture.

The four members

  • John Lennon 1940-1980
    Rhythm guitar, lead vocals. Lyricist of "Imagine".
  • Paul McCartney b. 1942
    Bass, lead vocals. Most-covered songwriter ever.
  • George Harrison 1943-2001
    Lead guitar. Brought sitar & Indian music West.
  • Ringo Starr b. 1940
    Drums; replaced Pete Best in August 1962.

A decade in seven beats

1960
Form in Liverpool; sharpen the act in Hamburg's clubs.
1963
"Beatlemania" sweeps Britain; "Please Please Me" hits #1.
1964
Ed Sullivan Show: 73M Americans watch live.
1966
Last commercial concert at Candlestick Park.
1967
"Sgt. Pepper's" — defining album of the psychedelic era.
1969
Final live show: rooftop of Apple Corps, London.
1970
"Let It Be" released; the band breaks up in April.
2023
"Now and Then" released — final Beatles song, AI-assisted.
