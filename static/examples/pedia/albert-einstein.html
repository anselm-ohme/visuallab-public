The 1905 Annus Mirabilis

Four papers, four foundations of modern physics — published when he was a 26-year-old patent clerk in Bern.

  1. Mar
    Photoelectric effect
    Light comes in quanta — laid the groundwork for quantum mechanics.
  2. May
    Brownian motion
    Statistical proof that atoms exist and have measurable size.
  3. Jun
    Special relativity
    Space and time are relative; the speed of light is the same for all observers.
  4. Sep
    Mass-energy equivalence
    $E = mc^2$ — the most famous equation in science.

Two iconic equations

Mass-energy
$$E = mc^2$$
A small mass releases vast energy — the principle behind both nuclear power and the Sun.
Field equations of GR
$$R_{\mu\nu} - \tfrac12 g_{\mu\nu} R = \tfrac{8\pi G}{c^4} T_{\mu\nu}$$
"Matter tells space how to curve; curved space tells matter how to move."

Confirmations of relativity over time

Major experimental tests, with the gap between prediction and confirmation in years.

Pacifism

A lifelong pacifist who emigrated to the US in 1933 to escape Nazism, yet signed the 1939 letter to Roosevelt that catalyzed the Manhattan Project.

Cultural icon

Time magazine's "Person of the Century" in 1999. The wild-haired physicist became the universal symbol of genius.

Quantum dissent

Famously skeptical of quantum indeterminism — "God does not play dice" — yet his 1905 work made the field possible.
