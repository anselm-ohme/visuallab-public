Olympic Games

Originating in Olympia, Greece around 776 BC and revived by Pierre de Coubertin in 1896, the modern Olympics bring together more than 200 nations and over 10,000 athletes every two years (alternating Summer and Winter editions).

Six moments that shaped the Games

1896 · Athens
First modern Olympics — 14 nations, all-male, 241 athletes.
1924 · Chamonix
First Winter Olympics held in the French Alps.
1936 · Berlin
Jesse Owens wins four golds; first global TV broadcast.
1968 · Mexico City
Black Power salute by Tommie Smith and John Carlos.
1992 · Barcelona
Post-Cold-War games; the US "Dream Team" debuts.
2024 · Paris
Opening ceremony along the Seine; first ever 50/50 athlete gender split.

Olympic Charter

"The goal of Olympism is to place sport at the service of the harmonious development of humankind, with a view to promoting a peaceful society concerned with the preservation of human dignity."

Most decorated athlete

Michael Phelps (USA, swimming) holds the all-time record with 28 medals (23 gold) across four Olympics from 2000 to 2016.
