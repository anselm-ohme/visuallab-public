Pieter Bruegel the Elder

About this work

Painted in 1565, The Harvesters is one of six (originally probably twelve) panels Bruegel made depicting different seasons of the year. Five survive; this one represents August, the ripening of summer.

Bruegel was the first European painter to make peasants the central subject of monumental art rather than incidental figures. The harvest is shown with humanity, humor, and a complete absence of moralising.

The Met acquired the painting in 1919 for what was then the colossal sum of $50,000. It is widely considered one of the greatest paintings of the Northern Renaissance and one of the Met's signature European works.

About the artist

Pieter Bruegel the Elder (c.1525-1569) was the most important Flemish painter of the 16th century and the founder of a multi-generational artistic dynasty. He painted only about 45 surviving panel paintings; the Met owns three.
